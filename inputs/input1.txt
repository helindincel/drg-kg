Göbekli Tepe is a Neolithic archaeological site in southeastern Turkey near Şanlıurfa. The site contains monumental circular enclosures with T-shaped limestone pillars decorated with animal reliefs. Archaeologists date its main occupation to the Pre-Pottery Neolithic period, roughly 9600 to 8200 BCE. Göbekli Tepe has changed scholarly views about the relationship between ritual, settlement, and the emergence of complex societies.
